# Behavioral Interview Prep — SDE 3

> **Format:** STAR — Situation, Task, Action, Result
> One polished story per principle. Adapt the same story to multiple principles where relevant.
> Target: Amazon LP (works for Google, Meta, Microsoft with minor framing adjustments).

---

## STAR Framework

```
S — Situation: Set the context (2-3 sentences, just enough)
T — Task:      What was YOUR responsibility? (not the team's)
A — Action:    What did YOU specifically do? (use "I", not "we")
               Go deep here — 3-4 concrete actions with reasoning
R — Result:    Quantify impact. What changed? What did you learn?
```

**Common mistakes:**
- Too much "we" → interviewer can't tell your contribution
- Vague results → "it went well" is not a result
- Story too long → 2-3 min max per story
- No failure stories → shows lack of self-awareness

## 14 Amazon Leadership Principles

---

### 1. Customer Obsession
> "Leaders start with the customer and work backwards."

**Story:** [Fill with your own — template below]
- **Situation:** Customer-facing bug / feature request / SLA breach
- **Task:** You owned the resolution despite it being outside your direct scope
- **Action:** Prioritized customer pain over internal convenience; changed design based on feedback; pushed back on stakeholder to protect UX
- **Result:** Metric improvement (latency, error rate, NPS, retention)

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you made a decision that wasn't popular internally but was right for the customer.
- Describe a time you went above and beyond for a customer.

---

### 2. Ownership
> "Leaders never say 'that's not my job'."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** System/process failing that wasn't strictly your team's problem
- **Task:** You stepped up without being asked
- **Action:** Diagnosed root cause, proposed + implemented fix, brought others in
- **Result:** Problem resolved, ownership culture strengthened

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you took ownership of something outside your job description.
- Give an example of when you saw a problem and fixed it without being asked.

---

### 3. Invent and Simplify
> "Leaders expect innovation and find ways to simplify."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Complex, over-engineered process or system
- **Task:** Needed to deliver faster / reduce operational burden
- **Action:** Proposed simpler architecture; eliminated unnecessary components; automated a manual process
- **Result:** Reduced complexity (lines of code, components, manual steps), faster delivery

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you simplified a complex process or system.
- Describe an innovative solution you implemented.

---

### 4. Are Right, A Lot
> "Leaders have strong judgment and good instincts."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Team or stakeholders wanted to go a different technical direction
- **Task:** You believed your approach was better and needed to make the case
- **Action:** Gathered data, prototyped, presented trade-offs — changed minds with evidence
- **Result:** Your approach was adopted, measurable positive outcome

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you had to make a difficult decision with limited data.
- Describe a time you were right when others disagreed.

---

### 5. Learn and Be Curious
> "Leaders never stop learning."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Needed to deliver in a domain you didn't know well
- **Task:** Ramp up quickly and contribute meaningfully
- **Action:** Deep dives, POCs, consulted experts, shared learnings with team
- **Result:** Delivered on time, team upskilled, became go-to for that domain

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you learned something new to solve a problem.
- Describe a time you pursued learning outside your comfort zone.

---

### 6. Hire and Develop the Best
> "Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and mentor others."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Junior engineer struggling, or hiring bar being lowered
- **Task:** Mentor / uphold bar / grow the team
- **Action:** Regular 1:1s, code reviews with teaching, design pairing, bar-raising in interviews
- **Result:** Engineer grew measurably; team quality improved; you multiplied your impact

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you mentored someone and helped them grow.
- Describe a time you upheld the hiring bar despite pressure to fill a seat.

---

### 7. Insist on the Highest Standards
> "Leaders refuse to accept mediocre work."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Team ready to ship something below quality bar (tech debt, security hole, poor UX)
- **Task:** You pushed back despite time pressure
- **Action:** Documented concerns, proposed targeted fix that didn't blow timeline, got buy-in
- **Result:** Shipped with quality; avoided a production incident that would have cost more

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you refused to compromise on quality.
- Give an example of raising the bar in code review / design review.

---

### 8. Think Big
> "Leaders create and communicate a bold direction."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Team solving an immediate problem with a narrow solution
- **Task:** You saw the bigger opportunity / longer-term vision
- **Action:** Proposed a broader solution that solved the immediate need + future-proofed
- **Result:** Delivered more value, influenced roadmap, team adopted the vision

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you proposed a solution that was bigger than what was asked for.
- Describe a time your long-term thinking changed the direction of a project.

---

### 9. Bias for Action
> "Speed matters. Many decisions are reversible."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Slow decision-making blocking progress (committees, approvals, analysis paralysis)
- **Task:** Needed to unblock the team without waiting
- **Action:** Made a reversible decision, shipped an MVP, ran an experiment — moved forward
- **Result:** Unblocked team, validated approach, course-corrected quickly if needed

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you made a decision without having all the information.
- Describe a situation where speed was critical and how you handled it.

---

### 10. Frugality
> "Accomplish more with less."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Budget/resource constraint that seemed to block a goal
- **Task:** Deliver the outcome anyway
- **Action:** Cut scope smartly, reused existing infra, automated instead of hiring
- **Result:** Achieved the goal within constraint; saved cost X

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you delivered more with fewer resources.
- Describe a time you found a cost-effective alternative.

---

### 11. Earn Trust
> "Leaders listen, speak candidly, treat people with respect."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Conflict with peer, stakeholder, or cross-team partner
- **Task:** Resolve without escalation, maintain relationship
- **Action:** Listened first, acknowledged their concern, found shared ground, gave credit
- **Result:** Alignment reached, relationship stronger, project unblocked

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a colleague and how you resolved it.
- Describe a time you had to give difficult feedback.

---

### 12. Dive Deep
> "Leaders operate at all levels and stay connected to details."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Production incident or mysterious bug
- **Task:** Find root cause, not just symptom
- **Action:** Traced through logs, metrics, code — went 5 layers deep; found non-obvious root cause
- **Result:** Real fix shipped; added monitoring to catch it earlier next time

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you dug into a complex problem at a deep level.
- Describe a time the details you uncovered changed the direction of a decision.

---

### 13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
> "Challenge decisions you disagree with. Once decided, commit fully."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** Team or manager decided on an approach you believed was wrong
- **Task:** Voice concern respectfully, then align once decision was made
- **Action:** Raised concern with data + alternatives; decision went another way; you committed fully and made it succeed
- **Result:** Showed you can influence AND be a team player; outcome was positive

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager or team.
- Describe a time you raised a concern and it was overruled — what did you do?

---

### 14. Deliver Results
> "Leaders focus on the right inputs and deliver despite obstacles."

**Story template:**
- **Situation:** High-stakes project with changing requirements / unexpected blockers
- **Task:** Deliver on commitment
- **Action:** Re-scoped smartly, escalated blockers early, rallied team, managed stakeholder expectations
- **Result:** Delivered on time (or with agreed adjustment); stakeholders satisfied

**Likely questions:**
- Tell me about a time you delivered a project despite major obstacles.
- Describe your most challenging delivery and how you managed it.

---

## Tips for SDE 3

- **Scope your stories at the right level** — SDE 3 stories should show cross-team influence, technical leadership, or mentoring. Pure IC "I fixed a bug" stories are SDE 1/2 level.
- **Have 5-6 core stories** that can be adapted to 3-4 principles each. Don't memorize 14 separate stories.
- **Metrics matter** — latency reduced by X%, cost saved by $Y, team grew from N to M engineers.
- **"Tell me about a failure"** — Pick a real one. Show what you learned and changed. Interviewers respect self-awareness.
- **Keep answers to 2-3 minutes** — Practice with a timer. Most candidates go 5-7 min and lose the interviewer.

---

## Google / Meta Adaptations

**Google:** Focus on "Googleyness" (collaboration, intellectual humility, comfort with ambiguity) + technical leadership at scale. Same STAR format.

**Meta:** Focus on "Move Fast", "Be Direct", "Build Social Value". Emphasize speed of iteration and data-driven decisions.

**Microsoft:** "Growth Mindset" (Satya Nadella) — learning from failure, empowering others, customer empathy.
